About

This webpage shows New York State and it's established Wildlife Management Units. Wildlife Management Units (WMU) are the Department of Conservation's geographical unit for setting hunting and trapping seasons.

According to the DEC, " Wherever possible, the boundaries between units were placed on County or State Highways or large streams or rivers making them clearly identifiable on the ground."

This Site has a few different buttons. Located at the top

HOME - WMUS - shows a simple map with each of the wildlife management units.

Deer Harvests - '88 - '23 - shows the estimated harvest data for every WMU from 1988 through 2023 of White Tailed Deer. Data is seperate into Male Adult, Male Fawn, Female Adult and Female Fawn.

Total Deer Harvests - '88 - '23 - shows the total estimated harvest data for every WMU summing all of 1988 through 2023 of White Tailed Deer. Data is seperate into Male Adult, Male Fawn, Female Adult and Female Fawn.

Bear Harvests - '70 - '23 - shows the estimated harvest data for every WMU from 1970 through 2023 for Black Bear. Please note if the data says "undefined" no data was present for that year.

Total Bear Harvests - '70 - '23 - shows the total estimated harvest data for every WMU summing all of 1970 through 2023 for Black Bear. Please note if the data says "undefined" no data was present for that year.

Bear Harvests by Method - '07 - '23 - shows the estimated harvest data for towns/citys in New York from 2007 through 2023 for Black Bear, for each type of season for Bear. Please note if the data says "undefined" no data was present for that year. Also note this data was developed from a PDF file, so numbers may not be accurate, or towns could be missing. This is just a repsresentation of what the data could look like. Actual data resides with DEC and the excel spreadsheet was not available at the time of making this map.

Annual Harvest Reports

Per an email conversation with the DEC, "DEC estimates the annual deer and bear harvest using harvest reports by hunters and physical examination of harvested deer and bear to determine the non-reporting rate"

Static maps are nice to look at, but maps like this one are more useful for someone looking for patterns.
